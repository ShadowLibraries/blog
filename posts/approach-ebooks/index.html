Creating ebooks in the pandemic: My journey into book piracy.

When the pandemic broke out in 2020, I finally had some free time. I had always been interested in book piracy, but had never managed to get my hands on it. Now I finally had a chance.

EpubLibre is probably the best site for downloading ebooks in Spanish. All the files published there and carefully crafted and reviewed by them. They are all created from scratch and the quality is excellent. So I decided to start my research there.

Interestingly, they have a 4-volume manual (in Spanish) that explains everything from the beginner’s guide to the most advanced stuff. And most importantly, they also provide an epub base file. With this in mind, I set about creating my first ebook. I had a Scribd account at the time, so I searched for books that weren’t available in some of the well-known shadow libraries such as LibGen, Trantor, etc. I copied the text from them and got to work.

To craft and edit the books I used Sigil, a free, open source, multi-platform ebook editor.

I ended up making 5 books,and uploaded them all to LibGen. Within a few weeks I found that they had been copied to other libraries, retaining the metadata I had included: “thekat” as the publisher. So I felt that I had finally done my bit to make some content available that wasn’t originally available.
